Each vertex is an admissible diamond; each leaf corresponds to a degeneration of MHS, you decompose the diamond into **blocks** i.e. irreducible $\mathfrak{sl}_2\times\mathfrak{sl}_2$ representations plus their conjugate representation (if they have one, self-conjugate representations don't get doubled), each of these irreps (and their conjugates) degenerate independently onto their respective limit by pivoting about their midpoint. A block thus get's sent onto a **pile** (of blocks). This naturally captures the lie theoretic fact that a limit of an irreducible representation is a sum of irreducible representations. This behavior is reminiscent of the behavior of sandpiles, as soon as we drop enough sand in the hodge vector the pile is allowed to fall (populate adjacent cells). However, since this decomposition into blocks is far from unique we capture this falling behavior by our tree. You have controls to examine the falling animation. Hover a node to replay its pivot.
